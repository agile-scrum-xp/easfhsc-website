The EAS Familial Hypercholesterolaemia Studies Collaboration (EAS FHSC)

Familial Hypercholesterolaemia (FH) is a common genetic disorder leading to lifelong elevations in LDL-Cholesterol, high cardiovascular risk and development of premature cardiovascular disease. Despite its frequency and preventable outcomes, FH is largely underdiagnosed and undertreated, and represents an unaddressed major public health problem that concerns countries worldwide. Joint efforts among multiple partners are key to reducing the burden of FH and closing knowledge gaps.

The EAS FHSC initiative is supported by the European Atherosclerosis Society and represents an international, worldwide, collaboration of stakeholders with an interest in FH. The mission statement of the EAS FHSC aims to empower the medical and global community to seek changes in their respective countries or organizations regarding how FH is detected and managed and the clinical consequences thereof, with a view to promoting early diagnosis and more effective treatment of FH.

The EAS FHSC global network of investigators will permit to generate robust information on how FH is detected, managed and the clinical consequences of current practices on outcomes. The information generated will help close gaps in knowledge, inform the best interventions, account for providing effective and efficient care, and consequently support the best evidence-based decision making in FH. Ultimately, this will aim to help prevent adverse outcomes and improve care of FH patients.

To ensure the smooth operation within the wider EAS FHSC global network, the EAS FHSC is supported by a number of National Lead Investigators/Country-leads. The Coordinating Centre for the EAS FHSC initiative is based at the Imperial Centre for Cardiovascular Disease Prevention (ICCP), School of Public Health, Imperial College London, UK.
